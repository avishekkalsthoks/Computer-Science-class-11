Chapter 7: Multimedia

Introduction

Multimedia consists of two words: 'Multi' and 'Media', where 'multi' means many and 'media' refers to the way or medium of expressing information. Hence, multimedia is the method of expressing information in various forms. The information can be expressed in text, audio, video, graphics, and animation. We use several forms of media for better understanding and effective communication. By using multimedia, any information can be illustrated in greater depth.

Since a single form of media is inefficient for better communication and understanding, we integrate several components of multimedia (text, audio, video, animation, and graphics) by using a multimedia computer system. This integration of different media forms is mandatory for effective communication.

A multimedia computer system consists of both multimedia hardware and software. Examples of hardware include printers, projectors, webcams, microphones, and digital cameras. On the software side, programs like text editors (MS Word), presentation tools (PowerPoint), photo editing software (Photoshop), video editing programs (iMovie), and accounting software (Tally) are widely used. These hardware and software tools work together to integrate multimedia components. For example, a video can be created by integrating audio and images.

Advantages/Merits/Pros/Importance of Multimedia

  • It helps make communication much more effective.
  • It enhances the level of understanding in any particular topic.
  • Games, cartoons, and movies are made more effective and realistic using multimedia.
  • It makes the teaching and learning process much more interesting and intuitive.
  • Virtual reality and simulations have become more realistic.

Disadvantages/De-merits/Cons/Limitation of Multimedia

  • Multimedia components may be expensive.
  • Technical knowledge and IT literacy are required to operate multimedia systems.
  • Technology is always changing and never constant.
  • Multimedia devices require electricity to function.

Components/Elements of Multimedia

Text

Text is the collection of alphanumeric characters or any symbols. It can be a word, a single line, or a paragraph. Text is created and customized using text editors or word processors like MS Word or Notepad. It is the basic way of expressing information. With multimedia software, text can be customized by adjusting size, color, style, and more. Common text document extensions include .docx and .txt.

Graphics / Images

The phrase "A picture is worth a thousand words" suggests that images can convey more information than text. Graphics refer to pictures or images, and the smallest element of an image is called a pixel (Picture Element). The quality of an image is determined by its resolution, which is the number of pixels it contains. Multimedia software like Adobe Photoshop, Corel Draw, and Adobe InDesign can be used to edit and create graphics. Common image file extensions include .jpeg, .png, and more.

Audio

Audio is an important component of multimedia that provides the sensation of hearing. It can be used to enhance understanding of a topic. Audio may consist of human voice, musical notes, natural sounds, or digitally synthesized sounds. Audio is transferred as a digital signal, and examples of audio software include Windows Media Player. Audio file extensions include .mp3, .wma, and more.

Video

Video is created by capturing a sequence of still images (frames) shown in rapid succession. The term "Frames Per Second" (FPS) is used to describe the smoothness of video. Typically, 15-25 FPS provides smooth motion, but 30 and 60 FPS are more common standards for devices. Video editing software such as iMovie, Final Cut Pro, and Filmora can be used to create and edit videos. Common video file extensions include .mp4, .mpeg, etc.

Animation

Animation is the rapid display of a sequence of computer-generated images (vectors) to create visual effects. These images are typically computer- generated and used to express complex ideas. Animations are widely used in the movie industry and other entertainment sectors. Animation can be 2D or 3D, with 3D animation being more realistic. Common animation software includes Maya, AutoCAD, and Macromedia Flash. Common animation file extensions include .flv, .gif, and more.

Application Areas of Multimedia

  • Education: Multimedia is used to create interactive educational content such as videos, animations, and simulations to enhance learning.
  • Communication: Multimedia improves communication by using text, audio, video, and images to convey information more effectively.
  • Entertainment: Multimedia is widely used in films, games, and other entertainment mediums to provide a richer experience.
  • Medicine: Multimedia aids in the visualization of medical data, simulations for training, and the presentation of complex information.
  • Science and Technology: Scientists use multimedia to illustrate complex concepts, create simulations, and present research data in a more comprehensible format.
  • Advertisement: Multimedia is used to create engaging advertisements that combine text, images, video, and audio to attract attention.
  • Websites/Webpages: Websites use multimedia components such as images, video, audio, and animations to improve user interaction and presentation.
